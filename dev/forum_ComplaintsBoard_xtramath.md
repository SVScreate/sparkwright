## Key Signals for Math Flash

- **Even defenders acknowledge the speed is "challenging and overwhelming"** — the positive reviews don't deny the stress, they argue it's worth it. This is a revealing framing: XtraMath's supporters accept discomfort as the cost of fluency. Math Flash's position is that discomfort is not a necessary cost — it's a design failure.
- **"Personalized learning" cited as a positive** — this reviewer likely means the adaptive difficulty, not per-student customization. Math Flash's teacher-adjustable threshold and per-fact tracking are more genuinely personalized than XtraMath's algorithm. Worth naming in positioning.
- **Copyright strikes on parody videos** — tells you something about the brand's relationship with its critics. Not directly useful for Math Flash positioning, but a cultural signal.
- **Small sample, low signal.** This source is less valuable than SmartCustomer or the LD Online thread. Included for completeness.
