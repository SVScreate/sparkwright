## Summary

A Change.org petition started January 15, 2020 by Evie Auger, directed at school principals, requesting they discontinue or reduce use of XtraMath. As of fetch date: 820 of 1,000 signatures. 67 documented supporter voices. The petition focuses on the 3-second time limit as the primary harm — characterizing the platform as creating excessive academic pressure with no engagement incentives.

---

## Petition Text (summary — full verbatim text not returned by fetch)

The petition argues that XtraMath creates excessive academic pressure on students. Core complaint: the app's strict **3-second time limit** for answering math facts, with a 6-second alternative available. The petition notes the platform provides no coins or prizes and focuses solely on rote memorization. The petitioner requests that school leadership discontinue or reduce use of the application to alleviate student stress.

## Key Signals for Math Flash

- **The 3-second timer is the named villain.** The petition does not object to math fact practice — it objects to the *implementation* of time pressure. This is the correct critique and it opens the door for a tool that uses timing more thoughtfully.
- **820 signatures toward a 1,000 goal** is modest — this is not a mass movement. But it represents real family frustration that surfaces in Google searches when parents are evaluating math fact tools.
- **No engagement = no buy-in.** "No coins or prizes" is a specific complaint. Parents and students want *some* sense of progress or reward. Math Flash's mastery system and (planned) Pet Fact system address this directly.
- **The target is principals, not developers.** This reveals where the frustration is directed: at the institutional decision to require the tool, not at the tool itself in isolation. Families who are *choosing* a tool for home use are in a different psychological position — they are opting in, which changes the dynamic entirely.
- **XtraMath's 6-second option is not mentioned favorably or even acknowledged as a solution.** Either families don't know it exists, or the UI makes it inaccessible, or 6 seconds still feels punishing in context. Worth investigating.
